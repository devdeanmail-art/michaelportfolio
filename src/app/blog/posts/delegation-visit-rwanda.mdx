In May 2024, I had the privilege of leading a United Nations delegation to Rwanda. The primary objective of our visit was to assess and enhance the operational readiness and aviation safety of the contingents deploying to UN peacekeeping missions, specifically focusing on the United Nations Mission in South Sudan (UNMISS).

## Collaborative Efforts

During our stay, we engaged in productive discussions with the leadership at the Rwanda Air Force Headquarters and the Rwanda Defence Force (RDF). Rwanda remains a steadfast and vital partner to the United Nations, consistently contributing highly trained personnel and critical assets to various missions.

## Assessing Capabilities

A key component of our visit involved evaluating the pre-deployment training and the maintenance of essential equipment. The dedication and professionalism demonstrated by the Rwandan forces are commendable and align perfectly with the UN's standards for operational excellence.

## Moving Forward

Our division, the Uniformed Capability Support Division (UCSD), is committed to ensuring that all Troop and Police Contributing Countries (T/PCCs) receive the necessary support and timely reimbursements. Visits like this are crucial for understanding the ground realities, building mutual trust, and streamlining our joint efforts toward global peace and security.

I extend my gratitude to the Government of Rwanda and the RDF for their warm hospitality and unwavering support to the UN's mandate.
