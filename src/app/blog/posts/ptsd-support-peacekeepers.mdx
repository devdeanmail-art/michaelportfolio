The physical and psychological toll of international peacekeeping cannot be understated. As the United Nations continues to deploy forces to some of the world's most volatile regions, the mental health of our personnel remains a paramount concern.

## A Landmark Initiative

In early 2026, the Uniformed Capabilities Support Division (UCSD) championed a landmark training program focused on Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) support for peacekeepers. This initiative is a critical step in recognizing and addressing the hidden injuries sustained during service.

## Supporting Our Forces

Our goal is to ensure that Troop and Police Contributing Countries (T/PCCs) are equipped with the knowledge and resources to support their personnel before, during, and after their deployments. The comprehensive PTSD study report, recently made available, highlights the necessity of proactive mental health care in maintaining operational readiness and force effectiveness.

## Looking Ahead

By institutionalizing these support structures, we are not only fulfilling our duty of care to the men and women who serve under the UN flag but also ensuring the long-term sustainability and success of our global peacekeeping missions. We will continue to advocate for enhanced medical and psychological support frameworks across all UN operations.
